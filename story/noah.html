Noah's Ark

A story of obedience, faith, and God's promises

Home › Stories › Noah's Ark

📖 Memory Verse

"I have set my rainbow in the clouds, and it will be the sign of the covenant between me and the earth."
— Genesis 9:13 (NIV)

A World That Had Forgotten God

Long, long ago — so long ago that you can barely imagine it — the world was full of people. But many of those people had forgotten about God. They were doing unkind, selfish things and the world had become a very sad place.

God looked down and His heart was heavy. But then He spotted someone special. A man called Noah.

Noah was different. He loved God with all his heart and tried every single day to be kind, honest, and good. God smiled when He saw Noah.

God's Big Instruction

One day, God spoke to Noah. He said something that must have sounded very strange at first:

"Noah, I want you to build a very large boat — an ark."

The ark was going to be enormous — longer than two football pitches and as tall as a four-storey building! God gave Noah very detailed instructions: how long, how wide, how tall, and what kind of wood to use.

Noah's neighbours must have laughed. There wasn't even a drop of rain in sight! But Noah didn't let that stop him. He trusted God and got to work. Chop. Hammer. Saw. Day after day after day.

"Noah did everything just as God commanded him." — Genesis 6:22

Two by Two

When the ark was finished, God told Noah to gather his family and bring two of every kind of animal on board — one male and one female. Can you picture it?

Big elephants and tiny beetles. Roaring lions and quiet little mice. Colourful birds and slithering snakes. Two by two they came, walking, hopping, waddling, and flying right into the ark.

Noah's family loaded food for everyone. Then they all climbed inside — and God Himself closed the great door.

Forty Days of Rain

Then the rain began.

It rained and rained and rained — for forty days and forty nights! The rivers burst their banks. The seas swelled. The whole earth was covered with water. But inside the ark, Noah, his family, and all the animals were safe and warm.

God had not forgotten them. He was watching over that great wooden boat the whole time.

The Rainbow Promise

After many, many days, the rain stopped. The water slowly went down. The ark came to rest on the top of a mountain called Mount Ararat.

Noah sent out a dove to see if the land was dry. It flew away, and came back with a fresh green olive leaf in its beak. That was the sign! The earth was ready.

God told Noah to open the doors and let everyone out. Out poured the animals — running, leaping, flying, splashing into the world. And Noah and his family stepped onto dry ground and thanked God with all their hearts.

Then God did something beautiful. He painted the very first rainbow across the sky — brilliant colours stretching from one horizon to the other.

"This rainbow is my promise," God said. "I will never flood the whole earth again. Every time you see a rainbow, remember that I love you and I keep my promises."

💛 What Did We Learn?

  • Obedience matters — Noah did exactly what God asked, even when it seemed strange.
  • God keeps His promises — The rainbow reminds us that God never forgets or breaks His word.
  • Faith means trusting without seeing — Noah had never seen a flood, but he trusted God completely.
